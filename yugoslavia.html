Yugoslavia a Toolof the West

„Yugoslavia came into existence and then disappeared exclusively according to the interests of Western countries,“ Karadžić wrote in 2010. During the twentieth century it was created and destroyed several times by whichever European power was at that time dominant.

The first instance was after World War I, when the Western allies determined that Germany was an intolerable threat to Europe, and resolved that it must be punished, kept down, via the Treaty of Versailles and wherever else they could find a way to subdue it. „After World War I, when Serbia was a victorious power that could determine what Serbia was going to look like, Croatia and Slovenia sought to enter the so-called Serbia, to unite, and the West saw an opportunity to cause Germany damage, to take Slovenia and Croatia from German influence, and to push Germany away from warm water ports on the Adriatic.“ They allowed the formation of Yugoslavia only as a tool to carry out their own intentions in Europe.

„When Germany prevailed over the Western allies in 1941, Yugoslavia disintegrated.“ The Axis powers carved up Yugoslavia and partitioned it between Bulgaria, Italy, Albania, Croatia, and Germany. They set the Yugoslav peoples against each other and armed the Croats and Muslims to exterminate the Serbs.

„When the Allies overpowered the Third Reich, Yugoslavia was restored in 1945.“ Now again the West punished Germany for the war, and they split it into a western and an eastern state, controlled respectively by the West and by the USSR (with which the the Western allies coӧperated temporarily, solely to subdue a mutual threat). The West and the USSR together determined to prevent it from regrowing power. Each shaped their portion in their own image and guided it toward themself: West Germany was rebuilt as a liberal-democratic satellite of America, and East Germany was brought into the ‘Communist Bloc’. In this arrangement, Yugoslavia was once again a tool to limit the influence of Germany, but as a neutral state controlled by neither the West nor the Soviets, it could, at best, be tolerated. To the USSR it was a rebellious liberal holdout which needed to be controlled, and to the West it was an untrustworthy, dangerous Communist country that had to be destroyed.

When Germany reunified (as it was never supposed to be able to do!) and was welcomed back among the European powers, now Yugoslavia was no longer a useful tool to the West, but only a Communist threat. And now the ethnic tensions growing therein presented to Europe the opportunity to topple another socialist state. „When Germany overpowered the Western Allies in 1991, Yugoslavia disintegrated. But now, in contrast to 1941, the contractors of this German plan are our allies from World War II. Well, this is what makes Germany's victory complete and definitive, that its opponents from World War II are carrying out its tasks that it failed to complete after it was defeated in World War II. This means that those governments are responsible. Yugoslavia was just a compass-needle, an indicator, the hand of a clock pointing to the mood in Europe and the current relationship between Germany and other European countries, rather than being a genuine need of its peoples.“

So Yugoslavia was only permitted to exist when it was useful to the current dominant European power.
