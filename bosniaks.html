Don’t Use the Term “Bosniak”

„„Bosniak“ is a loaded political and ideological term. People concerned with truth and civil rights should not be using it in its modern sense.

This is Bosnia, a historic region of the Balkans.

Many nations and religions are mixed here, but the main three nations are Muslims, Serbs, and Croats. This has been our homeland for many centuries, since before the Slavs ever became Christian or Muslim. And the peoples of Bosnia are all Bosniak, be we Muslim or Croat or Serb. Bosniak (bošnjak) is a geographical term, deriving from the region of Bosnia, and the river Bosna for which it is named. In accordance with Slavic language, the suffix ,-ak‘ will denote the place where someone is from, as in warszawiak, a person from Warsaw, or krakowiak, a person from Krakow. So it is with bošnjak: for all the term’s history up until the 1990s, bošnjak referred to a person from the region of Bosnia; it did not specify religion nor national identity. People identified with their nation more than their region. A Dalmatian Croat was foremost a Croat; a Kosovo Serb was above all things a Serb; and someone from Bosnia would be either Serb, Croat, or Muslim, before Bosniak.
